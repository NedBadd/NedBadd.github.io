Formula Student, my time as Head of Aerodynamics

Aerodynamic/ Mechanical Design and Manufacturing · 2021

Background on Formula Student

Formula SAE or Formula Student is a competition where (usually engineering) students from universities all around the world compete against one another to design, build, test and race a custom, open wheeled formula style race car. The competition features no wheel-to-wheel racing (for safety reasons) and is instead scored based on teams' and cars' performance across a range of events, spanning static, and dynamic events.

The competition was intended to reward teams for following good engineering practise, making decisions based on data, and fully understanding the technology on their cars.


The Executive Summary of Formula Student

I would argue that the biggest lesson in Formula Student is this: don't get bogged down in detailed design - you have to stay conscious of the bigger picture at all times. It's exceedingly easy as an inexperienced university student to want to go off and prove to yourself (and everyone in your team) that you are competent in the theory, and the tools you've just been taught.

FSAE is the place where you realise that the real world is a different place to your exercise book page, or CAD/ CAE. If you get lost in details, your timelines will slip, your team will have less time to fix all the things which will inevitably go wrong in early testing. Remember this car is custom made from the ground up by newly educated engineers. If your car doesn't work it's not going to be very fast.

The wise teams will be EXTREMELY cautious to not be overly ambitious with their design. Go for the lowest hanging fruit.

What you (an FSAE team member) get

At RMIT (at least while I was a student there) there was little to no academic or really any tangible benefit for your thousands of hours of dedication. Students were volunteers and gained nothing but experience for their trouble.

I'd argue however that this experience was the best of my engineering career so far. Total design freedom (at least within the rules) and a team of likeminded, motivated, and smart people to work with. Working on an FSAE team is like working at a startup, working on cutting edge technology. Communication is critical, you learn so much more than in classes.

It will be hard for me to do justice for the work I did in FSAE since I spent definitely thousands of hours spanning more than three years on RMIT's Electric Racing team (now called RMIT Motorsport).

First season, 2019

I joined FSAE in my fourth year at uni. When I joined, I was lucky to be able to demonstrate I was serious about getting stuck in early, and was invited to help on a lot of manufacturing. In 2019, RMIT Electric Racing was building the previous year's design, with the intent of ironing out the problems from the first build, and getting significant testing time.

I was lucky to join here, the 2019 team was exceptionally competent and organised.
