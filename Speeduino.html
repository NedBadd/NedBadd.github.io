Speeduino Build

Electronics · Late 2025

Overview

Speeduino is an open source ECU which consists of a Arduino Mega board, and a custom PCB which must have the components soldered by the owner/ builder.

The need

Mappable ECUs offer significant advantages for those who like to work on their own cars. In my case, the car I am building this for was released in the early 1980s, and hence ECU technology has moved on significantly since then. You have to be a bit mad to want to go to this level of DiY for a car, but if you're willing, Speeduino is shockingly good, and saves you hundreds if not thousands of dollars. Most importantly though, it's just really, really cool.

After working in electronics/ wiring for a year or so, I felt I had the skills to at least start tackling this project.

The case

The component kit I bought featured a 40 pin computer connector, and the speeduino board had pads integrated spaced to match that. That is fine but I didn't like the idea of having my car's reliability dependant on a connector which fundamentally was was designed to be used in a static, mostly vibration free environment.

Having had good experience with Deutsch connectors in both FSAE and at work, I decided it would be nicer if I integrated my case with three DTM 12-pin receptacles.

Testing with Tuner Studio

Tuner Studio is a free tuning environment which will be used to flash the tune parameters to the ECU once its complete. Much to my relief, after spending probably ~20 hours soldering and building, I plugged in my Speeduino and it powered and was attempting to read sensor data.

What is all this for?
